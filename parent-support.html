Parent FAQs

Questions parents often ask

Can a child with Cerebral Palsy improve over time?

Yes. While CP is a lifelong condition, it is not progressive — it does not get worse on its own. With consistent therapy, care and support, many children improve their movement, communication and independence.

When should therapy begin?

As early as possible. Early intervention takes advantage of the developing brain and often leads to better outcomes. If you have any concerns about your child's development, seek guidance promptly.

Is Cerebral Palsy curable?

There is currently no permanent cure for CP, but the right combination of therapies and support can significantly improve a child's quality of life and abilities.

How can I continue therapy at home?

Our parent training programs guide you through safe home exercises, positioning and daily-routine techniques so therapy continues between sessions. Always follow your therapist's specific advice for your child.

Is financial assistance available?

We provide guidance on government schemes and support options, and our donor-funded programs help families who cannot afford continuous therapy. Contact us to learn more.
